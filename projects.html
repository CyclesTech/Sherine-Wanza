Projects & Research

Why Most Students Fail Science in Secondary Schools

Description: This project investigates the factors contributing to the high failure rates in science subjects among secondary school students. By analyzing teaching methodologies, resource availability, and socio-economic influences, the research identifies key barriers to success and proposes strategic interventions to improve science education outcomes.

Key Focus Areas: Teaching methodologies, resource availability, student attitudes, socio-economic factors, educational interventions

Biodiversity of Marine Organisms

Description: This project explores the biodiversity of marine organisms in a specific coastal region, cataloging species diversity and assessing the health of marine ecosystems. The research contributes to conservation efforts by analyzing the impact of human activities and proposing strategies to protect marine biodiversity.

Key Focus Areas: Species cataloging, ecological health assessment, human impact, conservation strategies

Enhancing Student Engagement in Science through Digital Learning

Description: This research project examines the impact of digital learning tools on student engagement and performance in science subjects. By integrating interactive simulations, virtual labs, and digital resources, the study aims to identify effective strategies to make science more engaging and accessible for students.

Key Focus Areas: Digital learning tools, student engagement, virtual labs, interactive simulations, educational technology
